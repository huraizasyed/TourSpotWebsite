Culture
  • Language: The official language of Malaysia is Malay (Bahasa Malaysia), while English, Chinese dialects, Tamil, and indigenous languages are also widely spoken due to the country's diverse ethnic makeup.
  • Religion: The official religion is Islam, and the majority of Malaysians are Muslims. Other significant religious communities in Malaysia include Buddhism, Christianity, Hinduism, and traditional indigenous beliefs.
  • Cuisine: Malaysian cuisine is a delectable fusion of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous flavors, featuring dishes like Nasi Lemak, Satay, Char Kway Teow, and Rendang.
  • Celebration: Celebrations in Malaysia are vibrant and diverse, encompassing festivals such as Hari Raya Aidilfitri, Chinese New Year, Deepavali, and Wesak, reflecting the nation's multicultural and multireligious fabric.
  • Traditional Clothes: Traditional clothing in Malaysia encompasses diverse styles such as Baju Melayu, Baju Kurung, Cheongsam, Sari, Kebaya, and more, reflecting the rich cultural tapestry of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous communities.
Costs
It is better to book in advance to get lower prices.
  • Flight Tickets: The least expensive round-trip flights to Kuala Lumpur from LHR usually cost between £670 - £840.
  • Hotel: It cost from around £15 to £229 for a week's stay.
  • Food: Food costs £5 - £10 per day.

Overall you would need around atleast £20 to £239 per day, if you are not looking to stay at high-end hotel.

Places to Visit
  • Petronas Towers: Iconic twin towers dominating the city skyline, with an observation deck and a sky bridge offering panoramic views.
  • Kuala Lumpur City Centre (KLCC): A vibrant area featuring the Petronas Towers, Suria KLCC shopping mall, KLCC Park, and the Kuala Lumpur Convention Centre.
  • Chinatown (Petaling Street): A bustling area known for its lively street markets, Chinese temples, and vibrant atmosphere.
  • Little India (Brickfields): A colorful neighborhood offering Indian cuisine, temples, and cultural experiences.
  • Central Market (Pasar Seni): A cultural market showcasing Malaysian arts and crafts, textiles, and traditional souvenirs.
  • National Museum of Malaysia: Showcasing the country's history, culture, and heritage through exhibits and artifacts.
  • Merdeka Square (Dataran Merdeka): A historical square with the Sultan Abdul Samad Building and the Independence Square.
  • Jalan Alor: A famous food street in Bukit Bintang known for its diverse and delicious street food offerings.
Best Time to Visit
The best time to visit Malaysia is generally during the dry season, which occurs from late November to February, offering pleasant weather and ideal conditions for exploring various regions
