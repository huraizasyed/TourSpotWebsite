Culture
Azerbaijani culture is rich and diverse, reflecting the country's history, geography, and the mix of influences from various civilizations.
  • Language: The official Language is Azerbaijani, however Russian and English is also widely well known.
  • Religion: Majority of Azerbaijanis are Shia Muslims, and Islam has a significant influence on their traditions and culture.
  • Cuisine: Some of the popular dishes of Azerbaijan are plov, kebabs, dolma, and many more. For desserts baklava and halva are widely enjoyed, and tea is also essential to their lives.
  • Celebration: Novruz is the most significant holiday in Azerbaijan, celebrated by everyone. Other fesivals include Independence Day and National Flag Day.
  • Traditional Clothes: For men, it includes chokha, and various different embroided clothes for women.
Costs
It is better to book in advance to get lower prices.
  • Flight Tickets: During off-peak, economy flights would normally cost under £350. During on-peak, the costs can go upto £700.
  • Hotel: Hotel Rooms can cost from £20 to £200 per night for 2 adults. Prices can vary for different Locations, Room Type.
  • Food: On budget, food costs around £20 to £30 per day. If you are looking to go to luxury restaurants, it could cost above £100.

Overall you would need around atleast £40 to £230 per day.

Places to Visit
  • Flame Towers: Flame Towers are a group of three skyscrapers which are symbolised as fire, and are a reference to Azerbaijan's nickname "The Land of Fire".
  • Nizami Street: Nizami Street is a shopping street in the downtown of Baku. It has shops, cafes, and restaurants.
  • Fountain Square: This central square is a popular meeting point and often hosts outdoor events and entertainment.
  • Old City: It is most ancient part of Baku, which is surrounded by walls. It became the first location in Azerbaijan to be classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Baku Boulevard (Seaside Park): The square has often been the scene of public meetings, cultural gatherings, celebrations, ceremonies, parades, concerts and has lately become the venue for the city's extensive New Year celebrations. The boulevard contains an amusement park, yacht club and musical fountain, and various statues and monuments.
  • Maiden Tower: The Maiden Tower is an ancient structure in the Old City with a rich history. Visitors can climb to the top for panoramic views of the city.
  • Heydar Aliyev Centre: Designed by architect Zaha Hadid, this futuristic cultural center is known for its unique, flowing architectural design. It hosts exhibitions and cultural events.
Best Time to Visit
I would recommend Spring (April to June), you can enjoy without worrying about getting hot or cold. You can enjoy both outdoor and indoor activities.
