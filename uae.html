Culture
  • Language: The national language of UAE is Arabic. English is also widely known followed by Urdu and Hindi as Pakistani and Indians are the largest imigrant group in UAE.
  • Religion: Islam is the official and most widely followed religion in UAE by approximately 76% of the population.
  • Cuisine: Some of the cuisines thhat are widely eaten in UAE are Harees, Shawarma, Madrouba, Mandi, Ghuzi, Machboos, Maqluba, Kunafa.
  • Celebration: UAE celebrates several occasions from which main occassions includes National Day, Flag Day and Eids.
  • Traditional Clothes: Emariti women mostly wear abaya with hijab and/or burqa when going out, while being at home, they wear jalabiya/kaftan. Emiriti men wear Kandura with ghutra.
Costs
It is better to book in advance to get lower prices.
  • Flight Tickets: The least expensive flight usually costs around £300 to £500 for round trip. It can go upto £1000 depending on preferences and the time of the year your are travelling.
  • Hotel: It costs around £40 to £145 per night for 2 adults.
  • Food: Food costs around £10 to £40 per day. Food in UAE is easily accessible and not too expensive.

Overall you would need around £50 to £185 per day.

Places to Visit
  • Burj Khalifa, Dubai: It is tallest building in the world located in downtown Dubai. YOu can visit inside with a fees starting from AED 149 (around £32.28) to AED 769 (around £166.60) adults and can be booked on website.
  • Dubai Mall, Duabi: It is one of the largest mall in the world and the largest in Dubai. It is located beside Burj Khalifa.
  • Desert Safari, Dubai: A desert safari is a popular and exhilarating activity in the desert region.
  • Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, Abu Dhabi: It is the largest mosque in UAE with a spectacular architecture.
  • Ferrari World, Abu Dhabi: It is mostly indoors theme park located on Yas Island. It is the world's first Ferrari-themed park and features Formula Rossa, the world's fastest roller coaster.
  • Palm Islands, Dubai: It is a man-made island made in a shape of a Palm suggesting its name. There is also a hotel near the island that you can stay at.
  • Rain Room, Sharjah: You get rained on without getting wet. It is a great place for people in UAE as it doesn't rain in much in UAE.
  • Gold Souq, Sharjah: A place where you can buy all kinds of jewels.
Best Time to Visit
I would suggest to visit UAE in winter (December to January) as it is too hot in Summers and not that cold in winters. It would be the perfect time to enjoy all activities.
