Culture
  • Language: The Singapore government recognises four official languages: English, Malay, Mandarin, and Tamil. Mandarin is the most spoken language
  • Religion: Among Singaporean adults, 26% identify as Buddhist, 18% as Muslim, 17% as Christian, 8% as Hindu, 6% as a follower of Chinese traditional religions like Taoism or Confucianism, and 4% as some other religion, including Indigenous religions.
  • Cuisine: Singaporean cuisine is a delectable fusion of Chinese, Malay, Indian, and other influences, offering diverse dishes such as Hainanese Chicken Rice, Laksa, Chilli Crab, and Kaya Toast.
  • Celebration: Singapore celebrates a diverse array of cultural and religious festivals, including Chinese New Year, Hari Raya Aidilfitri, Deepavali, Mid-Autumn Festival, and Christmas, showcasing the nation's multicultural harmony through vibrant events, feasts, and traditional rituals.
  • Traditional Clothes: Singaporeans celebrate their multicultural diversity through a rich tapestry of traditional clothing, including the Cheongsam, Baju Melayu, Sari, and Kebaya, reflecting the nation's blend of Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Peranakan influences.
Costs
It is better to book in advance to get lower prices.
  • Flight Tickets: The least expensive round trip flights for LHR to Singapore usually cost between £620 - £830.
  • Hotel: On average, the hotel costs from £30 to £86 if you are not looking for for a luxurious hotel. If you are looking for a luxurious hotel, then the price can go upto £145+ per night.
  • Food: It costs around £30 for food per person per day.

Overall you would need around £230 to £800 per day.

Places to Visit
  • Marina Bay Sands: Iconic hotel and entertainment complex with a stunning rooftop infinity pool and observation deck.
  • Gardens by the Bay: A futuristic garden featuring the Supertree Grove, Cloud Forest, and Flower Dome.
  • Singapore Botanic Gardens: A UNESCO World Heritage site with lush greenery, themed gardens, and the National Orchid Garden.
  • Sentosa Island: A resort island with attractions like Universal Studios Singapore, S.E.A. Aquarium, Adventure Cove Waterpark, and more.
  • Singapore Zoo: A world-renowned zoo known for its open-concept enclosures and engaging wildlife shows.
  • Chinatown: A bustling neighborhood with vibrant streets, historic temples, and a variety of shops and eateries.
  • Little India: A colorful district known for its vibrant streets, temples, and diverse Indian culture.
  • Merlion Park: Home to the iconic Merlion statue, offering great views of Marina Bay and the city skyline.
  • National Gallery Singapore: Housed in the former Supreme Court and City Hall buildings, it showcases a vast collection of Southeast Asian art.
Best Time to Visit
February to April is generally considered a favorable time to visit Singapore.
